Jada Pinkett Smith teases appearance on The Equalizer with Queen Latifah after Chris Noths fir

Jada Pinkett Smith has reunited with Queen Latifah.

The 50-year-old actress took to Instagram on Sunday to share two shots alongside Latifah, 51, ahead of her upcoming appearance on The Equalizer next week.

The Red Table Talk star shared the images of herself to her 11 million followers posed with Latifah on the set of the crime drama, writing, ‘@queenlatifah x Jada = Trouble!’ with a happy emoji accompanying the post.

The latest: Jada Pinkett Smith, 50, has reunited with Queen Latifah, 51, with an appearance on the CBS crime drama The Equalizer set to air later this month 

In one shot, Pinkett Smith donned a textured black leather jacket while Latifah wore a black leather coat over an olive green top and black pants with a face mask.

In the second shot, Pinkett Smith wore a pink athletic jacket with maroon stripes down the arm and an off-white knit cap, while Latifah wore a blue and white knit sweater.

Pinkett Smith and Latifah have past worked together on multiple films, including the 2017 comedy Girls Trip, 2008’s The Secret Life of Bees (which Latifah appeared in and Pinkett Smith was an executive producer on) and 1996 thriller Set It Off.

On the show, Latifah plays the title role of ex-CIA operative Robyn McCall; in her appearance on the series, Pinkett Smith is set to play ‘a savant-level thief with a photographic memory’ named Jessie Cook who is ‘devious, unpredictable, and amoral,’ according to CBS.

Cook and McCall have past worked together, the network said, describing the dynamic between the characters as ‘oil and water.’

Pinkett Smith’s guest appearance comes two months after actor Chris Noth, 67, was fired from the series in December after he was accused of sexual assault by multiple women.

‘Chris Noth will no longer film additional episodes of The Equalizer, effective immediately,’ CBS said in a statement December 20.

Noth, who played former CIA director William Bishop on the show, issued a statement denying the accusations, saying they were ‘categorically false.’

Latifah addressed the issue on People (the TV Show!) last month, calling it ‘a dicey, delicate situation that requires a great deal of respect.

‘It’s still surreal,’ she said. ‘That’s a personal thing that he’s going to have to deal with.’

She said that she and producers on The Equalizer, which began airing last year, were ‘figuring out what we want to do creatively on the show and how we’re going to deal with that character.

‘Chris’s character’s obviously a big part of the show and it was amazing chemistry, amazing chemistry. And my feeling is justice has to prevail regardless. I just want the right things to be done, you know?’

The Equalizer featuring Smith is set to air February 27 at 8/7c on CBS, and streaming on Paramount+.
