15. Germany's Flat-Rate Brothels

Germany legalized prostitution in 2002, and it is now a multi-billion dollar industry. The brothels don’t all operate like those in the U.S., though. In Nevada, there is a very “a la carte” system, whereby the patron chooses a girl that will fulfill his desires for an agreed upon price. In Germany, there are “flat-rate” brothels which are basically like an all you can eat buffet! A patron pays an established amount of money, and he is free to partake in as much as he’d like with as many ladies as he would like. It’s Germany’s answer to the recession. Establishments that offer this service prefer to call themselves “all-inclusives.”

At the King George Club in Berlin, they charge $145 for all you can drink and as much “interaction” with all the girls you can handle. It is reported that some businesses in the city charge only $90. Most of the girls are reportedly from Eastern Europe, and work in the clubs for a year or two which guarantees a healthy rotation.

14. Twenty Brothels In The U.S.

Nevada is the only state in America to have legalized prostitution, and it has about 20 brothels. In its heyday in the mid-1980s, there were as many as 36. Some small brothels are in isolated “brothel towns” which, by law, cannot have more than 700,000 residents. Others are associated with big names like Dennis Hof, who owns the Moonlite Bunny Ranch and employs over 500 girls. Another caters to the after-party crowd. Others have strip clubs inside. Many boast that they are open 24-7-365.

13. Mentor Programs For New Hires

Brothels have a revolving door when it comes to their staff. Like any business, the owners want to make sure that their employees are well-trained and comfortable in their positions. The mentor teaches the newbie how to operate legally, as well as customer service. Sheri’s Ranch in Nevada even goes a step further, and offers education to potential clients. It’s not all work at U.S. brothels, though. Girls find that they have a lot of down time when they aren’t working during their 12-hour shifts. They are free to hang out on the premises, which often feature Jacuzzis, break areas and pools.

12. The House Doesn’t Supply Everything

Girls typically rent rooms from brothels whether they are in the U.S. or abroad. What may be surprising, though, is that some houses make the girls pay for incidentals such as toys, condoms and lubricants. Girls don’t always get free drinks at the bordello bar, either. This may be due to the fact that brothels have seen their profits go down over the years, even while they still have to pay for licensing, advertising, maintenance of the facility and keep up with rising medical costs. Or, perhaps they are just trying to discourage girls from drinking on the job.

9. Prices Are Not Listed

Women that work in brothels are employed as independent contractors. This means that they set their own prices. So, while brothels offer up a “menu” of services, you will not see prices listed in the U.S. By law, prices are negotiated with each particular girl. And we do mean negotiated – the girls don’t get paid if they don’t work, so they are usually agreeable to offering reasonable deals.

However, prices are far from cheap, since the house gets a cut. In Nevada, the prices are higher the closer one gets to Las Vegas (where prostitution is illegal).
