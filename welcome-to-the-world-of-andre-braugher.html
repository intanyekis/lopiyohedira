Andre K. Braugher: A Brief Biography

Andre Braugher, born on the 1st of July 1962 in South Orange, New Jersey USA, is a renowned film and television actor. He is most famous for his roles in TV series such as “Homicide: Life on the Street” (1993-1998) where he portrayed Frank Pembleton, and as Captain Ray Holt in the TV series “Brooklyn Nine-Nine” (2013-2017). His acting career took off in 1989.

Ever wondered about Andre Braugher’s wealth as of early 2017? Well, as per reliable sources, his net worth was estimated to be a whopping $8 million. This wealth was the fruit of his successful career as an actor, during which he bagged two Primetime Emmy Awards. Additionally, he also earned two nominations for the Golden Globe Award.

Andre Braugher’s Net Worth: $8 Million

Andre is the youngest child of four to Floyd Braugher and Sally Braugher. Floyd worked as a heavy-equipment operator while his mother was a postal worker. Andre pursued his education at St. Ignatius College Prep and then enrolled at Stanford University. Though he initially studied medicine, he later switched his major to theater and drama, ultimately earning his BA degree in 1984. Following this, he continued his studies at the Julliard Schools Drama Division, where he obtained a Master of Fine Arts degree in 1988, securing high honors by being named as the Most Outstanding Theater Student at graduation.

Andre’s acting career took off with his role as Detective Winston Blake in the television film “Kojak: Fatal Flow” in 1989, a role which he reprised in several sequels. After gaining widespread recognition, he starred in the Edward Zwick’s Academy Award-winning war drama “Glory” in 1989, and subsequently appeared in various films and TV series, solidifying his place in Hollywood and steadily increasing his net worth.

Notably, his role as Detective Frank Pembleton in the TV series “Homicide: Life on the Street” (1993-1998) proved to be a pivotal moment in Andre’s career. It earned him a Primetime Emmy Award in the category of Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series. Throughout the ‘90s, Andre also featured in notable productions such as the Academy Award-nominated mystery drama “Primal Fear” (1996) and the Golden Globe Award-nominated romantic drama “City of Angels” (1998).

Entering the new millennium, Andre’s career continued to flourish. He played various roles, such as Frank Pembleton in the television film “Homicide: The Movie” (2000) and Dr. Ben Gideon in the TV series “Gideon’s Crossing” (2000-2001). He continued to amass fame and wealth by expanding his portfolio with roles in other films and series, including his second Primetime Emmy Award-winning performance in the TV series “Thief” (2006), and a supporting role in the horror film “The Mist” (2007).

From 2009 to 2011, he starred in the TV comedy series “Men of a Certain Age” and also made recurring appearances in the TV crime investigation series “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit”. He then added to his repertoire by appearing in the short-lived but acclaimed TV series “Last Resort” (2012-2013) and securing the main role of Captain Ray Holt in the TV series “Brooklyn Nine-Nine” (2013-2017), for which he commanded a handsome pay of $100,000 per episode, having appeared in 81 episodes, a factor which significantly boosted his net worth.

On the personal front, Andre has been happily married to Ami Brabson since 1991, and the couple has three children.
