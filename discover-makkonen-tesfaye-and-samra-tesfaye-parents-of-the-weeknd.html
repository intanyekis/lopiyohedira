The Talent Behind The Weeknd   

Abel Makkonen Tesfaye, famously known as The Weeknd, stands as a prominent Canadian figure in the realms of music, showcasing his prowess as a singer, songwriter, record producer, and actor. Originating from Toronto, Tesfaye kickstarted his journey back in 2009, introducing his compositions anonymously on YouTube. He unveiled a trio of mixtapes in 2011: House of Balloons, Thursday, and Echoes of Silence. These mixtapes underwent a revamp and re-release on the compilation album Trilogy (2012).

Tesfaye garnered both critical acclaim and commercial triumph with his pop-infused second album, Beauty Behind the Madness (2015), securing the number one spot in the US. The album encompassed chart-topping singles on the US Billboard Hot 100 – “Can’t Feel My Face” and “The Hills” – clinching the Grammy Award for Best Urban Contemporary Album and scoring a nomination for Album of the Year. His following album, Starboy (2016), also soared to number one in the US, spawning hits like “Starboy” and “I Feel It Coming”.

Commendations for Tesfaye’s music echo its dark, immersive sound, drawing inspiration from genres such as R&B, synth-pop, and trip hop. His vocals have been lauded as “haunting” and “seductive”.

Beyond his melodic ventures, Tesfaye has delved into acting, featuring in several films and TV shows like Uncut Gems (2019) and The King of Queens (2010). As the brains behind the record label XO, he has signed artists such as Belly and Nav. Tesfaye stands tall as one of the prominent musicians of the 2010s, amassing an impressive collection of accolades including three Grammy Awards, 17 Juno Awards, and 30 SOCAN Awards. His global record sales surpass 70 million.

Tesfaye emerges as a global sensation, leaving an indelible impact on aspiring artists. His music resonates with its dark, captivating essence, while his vocals evoke a haunting allure. A true original, Tesfaye has reshaped the musical panorama.
